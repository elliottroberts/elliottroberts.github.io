About me

I am the Research Scientist – Ocean Acidification and Coastal Resilience at the Downeast Institute (DEI) in Beals, Maine. I lead laboratory and field-based research investigating how ocean acidification, warming, and other physicochemical stressors affect the life-history traits of commercially important shellfish such as lobsters, clams, and oysters. I am particularly interested in establishing a high-resolution monitoring network to detect changes in water chemistry that may influence shellfish health and distribution. I am also the founder of NuWa Solutions, a start-up focused on nature-based strategies to reduce atmospheric CO₂ through coastal enhanced weathering and blue carbon approaches. In partnership with DEI, NuWa is conducting laboratory-based ocean alkalinity enhancement (OAE) trials to examine how various alkaline minerals influence shellfish development and carbonate system dynamics across different sediment types. This work supports the development of MRV (monitoring, reporting, and verification) protocols for candidate deployment sites and lays the foundation for future field-based assessments of ecological interactions and carbon accounting. This work is complemented by broader scientific interest in the marine CO2-carbonate system, ocean biological pump/marine productivity, anthropogenic CO2 uptake, and terrestrial and ocean margin carbon export fluxes in the coastal ocean.
